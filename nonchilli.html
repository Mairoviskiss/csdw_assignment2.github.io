Pork in the orange 🚫 🌶

By Maíra Araújo

- Ingredients -

  • Pork belly (with a good belly layer 😋)
  • 1 orange
  • Salt flakes

    • Note: this piece of pork belly was about 700g.

- Method -

  1. Chop the orange in thin pieces;
  2. Use a piece of kitchen foil in the baking tray to wrap the pork;
  3. Place the orange pieces as a bed for the pork, at the bottom and at the sides of the foil;
  4. Put the salt flakes all around the pork meat;
  5. Put the pork on the "orange bed" with the belly facing up;
  6. If you want to, put a small bit of salt flakes on the belly, but be careful, as it is already salty;
  7. Wrap the pork meat leaving the belly without covering (do not forget to have enough foil to cover the belly after);
  8. Put it in the fridge for not less than 5 hours (if you can leave for 24hs would be ideal)
  9. After the period in the fridge, take the pork out and dry the belly as much as you can with a paper towel;
  10. Cover the entire meat (including the belly) with the kitchen foil and place the baking tray in the oven, within 200 degrees for approximately 40min;
  11. When you see it is cooked enough, open the foil to expose the belly, closing it as tight as possible to protect the meat;
  12. Increase the oven's temperature to maximum (around 250 degrees) and wait for around 10-15 minutes, or until the belly looks crunchy;

 Note: If you are wondering why putting the meat in the fridge, it is to dry the belly so it can be more crunchy. Same reason for the drying paper towel.
